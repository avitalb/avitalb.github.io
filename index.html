I am currently a Data Engineer at ActBlue, the preeminent small-dollar fundraising platform for progressive political campaigns and nonprofits.

I graduated from MIT with a Masters of Engineering in Computer Science. My thesis research centered on cybersecurity policy for municipal and local governments, advised by Lawrence Susskind and Daniel Weitzner. I helped teach Foundations of Internet Policy and Computer Systems Engineering. I also hold undergraduate degrees from MIT in Computer Science and in Urban Planning.

My technical expertise lies in backend software engineering and data engineering. I feel particularly enthused about working with geographic and civic data, building elegant data pipelines, and implementing robust engineering, testing, and documentation processes. I also have a background in technology policy with a focus on privacy and security through the lens of protecting individual rights.

I am passionate about using technology to fuel positive change, and I am excited about engineering and policy opportunities in impactful and mission-driven organizations in the private and nonprofit sectors as well as in government.

I enjoy long-distance hiking, experimental baking, reading (especially about geography and the outdoors), and spending copious amounts of time walking around and taking public transit.
